This is The Goon Squad

A Visit from the Goon Squad, which is authored by Jennifer Egan is a collection of stories that all deal with identity, time, and music. The chapters in this novel bounce around, and it’s weblike.

Meet the Author

Jennifer Egan is a well established American author. Numerous of her books are winning all sorts of awards. A Visit From the Goon Squad, for instance, won the 2011 Puiltzer prize. Fun fact: her recent novel Candy House is a follow up to this novel.

Themes

This novel have an abundance of themes, but here are the three main ones

Technology and Connectivity

Structurally, Egan’s novel is very much about how tightly connected characters' lives are. In some of the chapters, primary characters take the stage, and in others, they take a backseat.

Pop Culture

Throughout the book, Egan does a very deep critique of popular culture. Primarily, she focuses upon the music industry, but she ventures out into Journalism, Film, and photography.

Identity

Identity is arguably the most important theme in the book. Jennifer Egan dives deep, and explains that identity is inherent. Many of the characters struggle with finding their identity, and they are constantly exploring different avenues.

Meet Your Characters

Bennie Salazar

Bennie Salazar is a record producer for Sow's Ear Records. Arguably, he’s very successful, however, he distances himself from the people around him, and takes priority over his music. Bennie is a naturalist, and doesn’t believe in overproduction.

Sasha Blake

Sasha Blake is both a major, and minor character in the novel. She likes a wild life as she’s trying to uncover her true identity. She is concerned about time moving forward, and ageing. She is troubled by addiction, and stealing.
